Teachers questionnaires
- When did you started teaching?
- How long have you been teaching at this school?
- Do you see children who are playing games on their phone in the breaks/during class?
- Do you see a rising number in children whose free time spent on video games?
- Do you see a rise in aggressive behavior in children?
- Which games are students mostly talking about or what kind of games?
- When children are talking about meeting up outside of school do they talk about playing video games?
- Do you see a change in children's carrier choices or "dream jobs"? What are these changes?
- Do you see a difference in children reacting to violence or death regarding real life/historical? What are these changes?
- Do children try to use aggressive behavior to achieve something? Is it more often? Is it their first respond? 

Parents questionnaires
- How many children do you have?
- How old is your child? Boy or girl?
- How much do they play video games? (weekly)
- Do they play alone or with friends/siblings?
- Does the game have violence in it?
- Is violence the main goal of the game?
- Does the child spend money on it?
- Does the level of aggression change after playing?
- Did you notice any aggressive outburst during playing? (slamming the table, screaming, swearing)
- Is it a problem for the child to stop playing the game when asked?
- Is the child playing affects her/his sleeping pattern or studying?
- If you have more children do you see a difference in aggression which can be connected to the different time they spend on video games? -> maybe break up this question?
- Does your child tries to use aggressive behavior to achieve something?
- How does your child react to death or violence to important people to him? And towards strangers or people who are weaker than him? -> has to be broken up


Students questionnaires
- What games do you play regularly?
- How long do you play per day?
- How passionate are you about your games?
- Do you regularly spend money in-game? And how much?
- Did you cause physical damage to objects or people around you as an reaction to an in-game event?
- Do you get exited or happy when fighting in-game?
- Do you avoid or intentionally choose (competitive) shooters? (Fortnite, COD, Valorant, Counter Strike)
- Do you get mad when you get told to stop/ pause your play?
- How much time do you spend with your friends playing a video game? How much time do you spend with them outside of it?
- Do you play alone or with friends more?
- Do you play different games with your friends than when you game alone?
